KEY ASSUMPTIONS
Verified inputs and modelled projections underlying this financial plan
  • All salary costs fully loaded at ~30% Spanish Seguridad Social employer contribution (general regime).
  • AWS infrastructure verified at $59.35/month (April 2026, zero traffic) — scales to ~€300/month at 5 clients [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • SL constitution required in Month 1 before payroll, contracts, and invoicing can begin.
  • EU27 trademark registration initiated Month 1 alongside SL constitution.
  • Founder draws no salary prior to raise close — €0 pre-raise burn on salary.
  • First revenue: Month 8–9 (base) — single client at €5,000/month entry tier [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • Sales cycle: 6–18 months per institutional client (EU regulated financial institutions) [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • Pilot-to-paid conversion: 50–70% [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • GTM spend begins Month 4 — founder-led sales, no dedicated sales hire in funded period.
  • Legal retainer: €750/month from Month 2 (Audens — regulatory, DORA, investor docs).
  • Gross margin: 75–85% at scale (Bessemer/OpenView 2023 infrastructure SaaS benchmark) [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • No token, no balance sheet exposure, no banking licence required.
  • Raise base case: €900,000 convertible note — provides ~26–28 months runway at base burn [MODELLED ASSUMPTION].
  • €4,500 pre-raise founder personal investment not included in raise proceeds.
  • Revenue projections are directional targets — validation pending first pilot conversion.
  • Collections sensitivity: Base burn model assumes client payments received in the month of invoice. In EU institutional sales, payment terms of 30–60 days are standard; large regulated institutions can exceed 90 days. At €5,000/month per client and up to 5 active clients by Month 18 [MA], a full 60-day payment delay represents approximately €50,000 of working-capital timing — under 6% of the €900K raise and covered by the existing runway buffer. A dedicated monthly cash-flow statement with collections timing will be produced post-SL constitution, once invoicing begins.
Summary: €900K convertible note at €5M–€6M cap. 75–85% gross margin target at scale. No token exposure, no banking licence required. All costs fully loaded with Spanish employer contributions. Revenue projections are directional — validation pending.

RUNWAY PROJECTION
Cash position trajectory · €900K raise · Base scenario

At base burn, the €900,000 raise provides approximately 26–28 months of runway [MODELLED ASSUMPTION] after accounting for the Month 1 legal one-off costs. The raise is deliberately sized above the 18-month minimum (approximately €426K net cash needed over 18 months) to provide a substantial buffer for: sales cycle slippage inherent in EU institutional sales, second client acquisition costs, mainnet migration expenses, and — critically — a strong Series A negotiating position. The company does not intend to raise from a position of desperation.

The company reaches first revenue at Month 8–9 [MODELLED ASSUMPTION], meaningfully reducing net burn from that point forward. Series A trigger metrics include 5–10 paying institutional clients, €400K–€800K ARR, and mainnet deployed. The pre-seed raise is structured to fund the company through to a position where these metrics are unambiguously met — leaving approximately €470K+ in the bank at Month 18 [MODELLED ASSUMPTION], with ample runway to negotiate Series A from strength.
