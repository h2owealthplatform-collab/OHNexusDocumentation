The root cause is structural — not technological.
CAUSE
European financial regulations (MiFID II, DORA, eIDAS 2.0, AML) require every institution to independently verify user identity and compliance for every interaction. There is no shared trust layer.
PROBLEM
Banks, EMIs and FinTechs build and maintain isolated, non-standardised point-to-point integrations. KYC, AML and MiFID II processes are repeated from scratch with every provider — by every institution — at massive cost.
CONSEQUENCE
$85B wasted annually across EMEA in duplicated financial crime compliance processes. Average institution: $72.9M/year. Germany alone: $32.5B. France: $25.3B.
Source: LexisNexis Risk Solutions, 2024
The affected party: Banks, EMIs, FinTechs and asset managers operating in the EU regulated financial ecosystem.

Why now? Three regulations demand action before 2027.
eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet
MANDATORY 2026–2027
EU Regulation 2024/1183. Active May 2024. Every EU member state must deploy EUDI digital identity wallets by 2026–2027. Every bank and FinTech must adapt their identity layer. This is not optional. STRONGEST regulatory pull in EU digital identity history.
DORA Enforcement
ACTIVE JAN 2025
Digital Operational Resilience Act. Mandatory from January 2025. Requires cryptographic audit trails and auditable ICT infrastructure across all regulated EU financial entities. Non-compliance: supervisory sanctions.
PSD3 / PSR + MiFID II
2025–2027
PSD3/PSR expanding Open Banking into Open Finance. 2,500+ TPPs already registered under PSD2. MiFID II structural compliance burden ongoing. Combined: every financial interaction must be traceable, auditable and provable on demand.
The compliance window is open now. Institutions that delay face regulatory penalties and operational disruption.
