RUNWAY PROJECTION
Cash position trajectory · €900K raise · Base scenario

At base burn, the €900,000 raise provides approximately 26–28 months of runway [MODELLED ASSUMPTION] after accounting for the Month 1 legal one-off costs. The raise is deliberately sized above the 18-month minimum (approximately €426K net cash needed over 18 months) to provide a substantial buffer for: sales cycle slippage inherent in EU institutional sales, second client acquisition costs, mainnet migration expenses, and — critically — a strong Series A negotiating position. The company does not intend to raise from a position of desperation.

The company reaches first revenue at Month 8–9 [MODELLED ASSUMPTION], meaningfully reducing net burn from that point forward. Series A trigger metrics include 5–10 paying institutional clients, €400K–€800K ARR, and mainnet deployed. The pre-seed raise is structured to fund the company through to a position where these metrics are unambiguously met — leaving approximately €470K+ in the bank at Month 18 [MODELLED ASSUMPTION], with ample runway to negotiate Series A from strength.
